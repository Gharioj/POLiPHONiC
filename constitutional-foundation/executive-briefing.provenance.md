## Extraction method

Same method as the Creator Constitution integration: the .docx was unzipped as OOXML and text recovered by stripping tags from `word/document.xml`, split on paragraph boundaries. Formatting (emphasis, tables, images, if any) is not preserved by this method and was not cross-checked against a normal rendering of the file.

## Constitutional dependency selected

**Executive Briefing** — selected over the other three documents the Creator Constitution names as its source (Technical Architecture, Governance Charter, Founding Partners Charter) because, unlike those three, the Executive Briefing carries no "Constitutional Provenance" section citing an upstream document. Its own text states it "presents the constitutional principles upon which that infrastructure has been conceived" — it is the root of the dependency chain, not a derivation of it.

This was confirmed, not assumed: the other three documents' own opening sections were read to check their stated dependencies —
- Technical Architecture: "derived from the constitutional principles established within the Executive Briefing."
- Governance Charter: "derived from the constitutional principles established within the Executive Briefing," building on the Technical Architecture.
- Founding Partners Charter: "derived from the constitutional principles established within the Executive Briefing, the Technical Architecture and the Governance Charter."

All three converge on the Executive Briefing as their common root. Integrating it resolves the deepest, most-shared gap in the Foundation.

## Constitutional judgements made during integration

1. **Structure preserved as authored.** The eight numbered sections, the five "Foundational Principles" (rendered as bold lead-ins), and the six "Platform Overview" capabilities (Recognition, Authorship, Collaboration, Rights, Economic Participation, Legacy) were kept in their original order and grouping — no section was reordered, merged, or retitled.
2. **Wording preserved verbatim**, as with the Creator Constitution — no paraphrasing or condensing.
3. **Publication Information retained but separated**, consistent with the Creator Constitution's treatment, as source metadata rather than constitutional substance.
4. **No other cited document integrated.** The Executive Briefing itself makes no forward reference requiring the other three documents to be read alongside it — it is self-contained by design, which is part of why it was judged the correct next dependency.
