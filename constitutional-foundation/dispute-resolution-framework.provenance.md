## Remaining constitutional questions

- **Same recurring gap, a third time.** This framework describes what dispute resolution must be (fair, transparent, impartial, evidence-based, reconciliation-oriented) but never names who hears a dispute, what forum or panel makes a determination, or what happens if a determination is itself contested. §4 requires determinations be "independently reviewable" without saying by whom.
- One new, genuinely constitutional observation: §2 states dispute resolution's purpose is explicitly "not to create new constitutional rights or obligations" — it only "reaffirms" existing principles. This means dispute resolution cannot, by its own terms, fill the gaps already identified elsewhere (e.g. it cannot itself settle the rights-vs-ownership question from the Creator Constitution, since doing so would functionally create a new determination of what a right is, not merely reaffirm one). That tension — a document that can only reaffirm, applied to a corpus containing unresolved ambiguities it cannot itself resolve — is left open.
- Rights vs. ownership (Creator Constitution) remains untouched.
