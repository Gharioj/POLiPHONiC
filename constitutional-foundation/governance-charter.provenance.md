## Unresolved constitutional questions

- The Charter repeatedly asserts that governance "derives its authority from the Constitution" and that no body may hold constitutional authority independently of it, but the document never names or defines the actual governance body, council, or decision-making entity that exercises this authority in practice — nor does its Constitutional Amendments section (§8) specify who may propose an amendment or how one is ratified. This is a genuine constitutional gap, not a formatting one: authority and accountability are asserted, but the "who" is undefined. It is possible the still-unintegrated Founding Partners Charter answers this — that dependency is noted but not assumed. Indexed as [Constitutional Discovery 003](../constitutional-discoveries/003-undefined-governance-authority-and-process.md).
