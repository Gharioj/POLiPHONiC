# Constitutional Discovery 003

## Discovery

Multiple documents in the Constitutional Foundation assert that governance authority derives from the Constitution, and that decisions, amendments, disputes and succession must proceed through defined constitutional processes — but no document names the actual governance body, decision-making entity, amendment procedure, dispute-resolution forum, or custodial succession procedure that exercises this authority in practice.

## Trail

- **First identified**, integrating the Governance Charter (v0.5.0): "The Charter repeatedly asserts that governance 'derives its authority from the Constitution' and that no body may hold constitutional authority independently of it, but the document never names or defines the actual governance body, council, or decision-making entity that exercises this authority in practice — nor does its Constitutional Amendments section (§8) specify who may propose an amendment or how one is ratified. ... It is possible the still-unintegrated Founding Partners Charter answers this — that dependency is noted but not assumed." — [`governance-charter.provenance.md`](../constitutional-foundation/governance-charter.provenance.md)
- **Confirmed as load-bearing**, integrating the Founding Partners Charter (v0.6.0): "This document now depends on that same undefined mechanism a second time — the gap first flagged when integrating the Governance Charter remains open, and this integration confirms it is load-bearing rather than incidental: two backbone documents now presuppose a governance process that is not yet constitutionally defined anywhere in the Foundation." — [`founding-partners-charter.provenance.md`](../constitutional-foundation/founding-partners-charter.provenance.md)
- **Reinforced by the document selected to resolve it**, integrating the Governance Procedures Framework (v0.8.0), which had been recommended specifically to "support governance authority, decision-making, amendments, dispute resolution and constitutional accountability": "it does not name a governance body, decision-making entity, amendment procedure, or dispute-resolution mechanism anywhere. It operates entirely at the level of principle ... The gap identified across the last three reviews (no named governance body or concrete process) remains fully open after this integration." — [`governance-procedures-framework.provenance.md`](../constitutional-foundation/governance-procedures-framework.provenance.md)
- **Amendment procedure specifically confirmed undefined**, integrating the Amendment Framework (v0.9.0): "confirms the same undefined-mechanism gap found in the Governance Procedures Framework (amendment criteria are stated, but no proposer, approving body, or ratification step is named)." — [`CHANGELOG.md`](../CHANGELOG.md), v0.9.0
- **Dispute-resolution mechanism specifically confirmed undefined**, integrating the Dispute Resolution Framework (v0.10.0): "confirms the recurring undefined-mechanism gap (no forum or panel named)." — [`CHANGELOG.md`](../CHANGELOG.md), v0.10.0
- **Extended to custodial succession**, integrating the Custodianship Framework (v0.22.0): "No custodian, custodial body, or succession procedure is named anywhere in the Foundation." — [`CHANGELOG.md`](../CHANGELOG.md), v0.22.0

## Status

Open. Naming who holds governance authority, and by what process it is exercised, is a constitutional judgement — it is not made here.

*Note, 2026-07-20:* newly staged material touches this question in principle terms without naming a concrete mechanism — see [`constitutional-staging/2026-07-20-constitutional-working-session.md`](../constitutional-staging/2026-07-20-constitutional-working-session.md), §5, §11 and §12. It has not been reviewed or adopted as a Resolution, so this discovery remains open.
