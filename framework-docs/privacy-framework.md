# POLiPHONiC — Constitutional Privacy Framework

*Constitutional Infrastructure for Creativity — Protecting Constitutional Privacy Whilst Preserving Constitutional Trust.*

## Constitutional Provenance

This publication forms part of the POLiPHONiC Constitutional Library.

It is derived from the Executive Briefing, the Technical Architecture, the Governance Charter, the Founding Partners Charter, the Creator Constitution, the Technical Implementation Specification, the Legal & Constitutional Framework, the Economic Participation Framework, the Constitutional Standards Framework, the Constitutional Operations Framework, the Constitutional Assurance Framework, the Constitutional Interoperability Framework, the Constitutional Innovation Framework, the Constitutional Trust Framework, the Constitutional Identity Framework, the Constitutional Data Framework, the Constitutional Provenance Framework, the Constitutional Collaboration Framework, the Constitutional Rights Framework, the Constitutional Authorship Framework, the Constitutional Recognition Framework, the Constitutional Legacy Framework, the Constitutional Creative Compacts Framework and the Constitutional Security Framework.

Its purpose is to establish the constitutional framework through which privacy is recognised, protected and preserved throughout the Constitutional Infrastructure.

Accordingly, privacy is not merely a legal obligation. Privacy is the constitutional protection of individuals, their recognised participation and the constitutional relationships through which they contribute to creativity. Privacy and transparency are therefore complementary constitutional principles. Transparency preserves constitutional confidence. Privacy preserves constitutional dignity.

## Executive Overview

Trust depends upon transparency. Transparency depends upon accountability. Accountability depends upon responsible stewardship of information. The Constitutional Infrastructure exists to preserve recognised creative relationships without compromising the dignity, autonomy or privacy of those who participate within it.

The Constitutional Privacy Framework establishes the enduring principles through which personal information, constitutional records and recognised participation remain protected whilst enabling transparency, collaboration and constitutional continuity. Privacy therefore strengthens trust by protecting people as well as constitutional relationships.
