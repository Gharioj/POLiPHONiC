# POLiPHONiC — Constitutional Rights Framework

*Constitutional Infrastructure for Creativity — Establishing the Constitutional Framework for Creative Rights.*

## Constitutional Provenance

This publication forms part of the POLiPHONiC Constitutional Library.

It is derived from the Executive Briefing, the Technical Architecture, the Governance Charter, the Founding Partners Charter, the Creator Constitution, the Technical Implementation Specification, the Legal & Constitutional Framework, the Economic Participation Framework, the Constitutional Standards Framework, the Constitutional Operations Framework, the Constitutional Assurance Framework, the Constitutional Interoperability Framework, the Constitutional Innovation Framework, the Constitutional Trust Framework, the Constitutional Identity Framework, the Constitutional Data Framework, the Constitutional Provenance Framework and the Constitutional Collaboration Framework.

Its purpose is to establish the constitutional framework through which creative rights are recognised, governed and preserved throughout the complete lifecycle of creative work.

Accordingly, rights do not exist independently of creativity. Rights are derived from recognised participation, enduring authorship and transparent collaboration. Every constitutional right therefore remains continuously connected to the constitutional relationships from which it is established.

## Executive Overview

Rights do not begin with legal instruments. They begin with creativity. Recognition establishes authorship. Authorship enables collaboration. Collaboration defines rights. Rights therefore become the constitutional expression of recognised creative relationships rather than isolated legal entitlements.

The Constitutional Rights Framework establishes the enduring principles through which creative rights remain transparent, accountable and continuously connected to constitutional purpose.
