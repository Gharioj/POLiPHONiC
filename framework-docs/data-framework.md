Its purpose is to establish the constitutional framework through which data is recognised, governed, protected and preserved throughout the Constitutional Infrastructure.

Accordingly, data is not treated as a technical asset. Data is the constitutional record through which recognised participation, authorship, collaboration, rights, economic participation and legacy remain continuously connected. ## Executive Overview

The Constitutional Infrastructure depends upon trusted constitutional records. Identity establishes recognition. Recognition establishes authorship. Authorship enables collaboration. Collaboration defines rights. Rights enable economic participation. Economic participation contributes to legacy. Data preserves each of these constitutional relationships throughout the complete lifecycle of creative work.
