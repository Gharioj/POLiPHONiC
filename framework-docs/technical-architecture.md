## 2. Identity & Recognition

**Constitutional Principle**
Creativity seeks Recognition. Recognition establishes the constitutional origin of every creative relationship. Without recognised participation there can be no trusted authorship. Without trusted authorship there can be no enduring collaboration, rights or economic participation. Recognition therefore forms the constitutional foundation of the POLiPHONiC architecture.

**Architectural Objective**
The architecture establishes a trusted identity layer through which recognised creative participation is established from the moment participation begins. Identity exists independently of individual works. Recognition exists independently of commercial relationships. Together they establish the constitutional foundation from which every subsequent creative relationship is derived.

**Architectural Design**
The Identity & Recognition Architecture serves four constitutional objectives.
- Establish trusted creator identity.
- Establish recognised creative participation from the moment participation begins.
- Preserve continuity between recognised participation and authorship.
- Maintain enduring continuity between recognised participants and their complete creative record.

Together these objectives establish constitutional continuity between participation, recognition and authorship.

**Technical Implementation**
Implementation provides authenticated identity, cryptographic verification, immutable participation records and verifiable attribution. These capabilities establish trusted recognition throughout the complete lifecycle of creative work.

**Constitutional Outcome**
Recognition establishes the constitutional origin of every creative relationship.
