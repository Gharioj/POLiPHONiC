## Points a reviewer will need to address before any integration

None of the following is resolved or judged here — each is a pointer for whoever performs that review.

1. **Overlaps Discovery 001** (rights vs. ownership undefined). §4 (Constitutional Rights) and §10 (Ownership) assert that constitutional rights derive from participation, not ownership, and that "ownership is not the source of constitutional rights." This speaks directly to the question [Discovery 001](../constitutional-discoveries/001-rights-vs-ownership-undefined.md) records as open. It has not been adopted as a Resolution, so Discovery 001 remains open per this repository's own convention (open until a Resolution addresses it) — but a reviewer should read the two side by side.
2. **Touches Discovery 003** (undefined governance authority and process). §5 (Hierarchy), §11 (Constitutional Responsibility) and §12 (Constitutional Authority) describe how authority is earned and recognised in principle terms ("cultivated through constitutional culture," "recognised by the constitutional community, not merely claimed... or conferred by position") — but, like every Foundation document [Discovery 003](../constitutional-discoveries/003-undefined-governance-authority-and-process.md) already cites, it still does not name a concrete governance body, decision-making entity, or process. It restates the existing principle-without-mechanism pattern rather than closing it.
3. **Reuses names already defined in the integrated Foundation.** §3 (Authorship) and [`constitutional-foundation/authorship-framework.md`](../constitutional-foundation/authorship-framework.md) both define "Authorship" independently and in different words. The same is true of §4 (Constitutional Rights) against [`constitutional-foundation/rights-framework.md`](../constitutional-foundation/rights-framework.md), and §8 (Constitutional Custodianship) against [`constitutional-foundation/custodianship-framework.md`](../constitutional-foundation/custodianship-framework.md). Whether these are consistent restatements or competing definitions has not been assessed here.
4. **Introduces terms with no existing Foundation counterpart at all**: Constitutional Contribution, Constitutional Value, Constitutional Hierarchy, Constitutional Community, Constitutional Participation (distinct from the already-integrated *Economic* Participation Framework), Constitutional Stewardship, Constitutional Culture, Constitutional Space. These would be wholly new vocabulary if integrated.
5. **Internally self-referential.** The Hierarchy (§5) and both Progressions (§9, §12) each depend on definitions given elsewhere in this same submission — the fourteen sections are not independent and should be reviewed as one system, not piecemeal.
